About ClearSource

Making water quality data accessible to everyone

Our Mission

ClearSource helps communities easily access and understand information about local water quality. We take complex scientific data from the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) and present it in a simple, user-friendly format.

Understanding Water Quality

pH Level: This measures how acidic or alkaline water is on a 0-14 scale. Safe drinking water falls between 6.5 and 8.5, with 7 being neutral. If pH drops below 6.5, the water becomes acidic and may corrode pipes. When it rises above 8.5, you might notice a bitter taste.

Temperature: Most healthy aquatic ecosystems thrive between 50-77°F (10-25°C). Extreme readings, whether below freezing or above 85°F, can stress wildlife and may signal environmental problems.

Discharge: Think of this as the river's flow rate, measured in cubic feet per second. What's "normal" varies dramatically by location and season. A sudden spike might mean flooding or heavy rain upstream, while a drop could indicate drought. Compare current readings to that site's historical averages to know if something's unusual.

Turbidity: This tells you how clear the water is. Crystal-clear water measures below 5 NTU (Nephelometric Turbidity Units). Readings between 5-50 show some cloudiness. Anything above 50 suggests high pollution, sediment, or algae: and the water may not be safe.

Our Data Source

All water quality data comes from the U.S. Geological Survey (USGS) Water Services API. The USGS operates thousands of monitoring stations across the United States, collecting real-time data on water conditions.

Color-Coded Safety Indicators

Green (Safe) Water quality is within normal, safe ranges

Yellow (Moderate) Water quality shows some concern, use caution

Red (Unsafe) Water quality is outside safe ranges, avoid contact
